WE ARE VOLCANO-TASTY

Masaya is a Volcano located in Nicaragua, which is also known as "The land of lakes and volcanoes”. Among the dozens of things to see in Nicaragua, here is one of the few places in the world where you can observe lava up close. The Masaya volcano, located in the city of the same name, is one of the most active in the country and since 2016 and you can see the actual Lava in the Santiago crater flowing inside it.
Visiting the Masaya Volcano National Park is best at sunset. The visitor wouldn't expect this: Just a few steps from your parked car, you could easily contemplate this natural phenomenon that'll leave you speechless!. This trip can be arranged from Granada or Managua, both about one hour away by car.

Pio Quinto

Taste of Precious

Every Nicaraguan Christmas table will not be complete without one of the most famous Nicaraguan desserts, Pio Quinto. Believed to be named after Pope Pius V, this Christmas cake is very similar to the Latin American favorite tres leches. The main difference is that instead of milk, Pio Quinto is soaked in rum giving it a distinct flavour that Nicaraguans—and even tourists—truly love. The traditional Pio Quinto recipe uses a combination of flour and white cornmeal called "Pinol". Along with it is baking powder, milk and vanilla white the syrup is with from sugar, cinnamon stick, lime juice, rum, whole cloves, raisins and prunes. Pio Quinto is usually topped with traditional custard made with cinnamon, sugar, egg yolk and cornstarch. The cake is completed with a generous dusting of cinnamon on top. Pio Quinto is served after every Christmas meal in Nicaragua and this tradition continues to be practiced today.
